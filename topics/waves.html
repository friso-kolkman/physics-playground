The Physics

Waves combine through superposition — overlapping waves add their heights at every point. When two waves share the same frequency, the result depends on their phase relationship: perfectly in sync gives constructive interference (double the height), perfectly out of sync gives destructive interference (they cancel). When the frequencies differ slightly, you hear beats — a periodic pulsing as the waves drift in and out of phase.

Resonance is what happens when you push a system at its natural frequency. Every object has a frequency at which it naturally vibrates — a swing, a bridge, a wine glass. Push at that frequency and the amplitude grows dramatically, because each push arrives at exactly the right moment to add energy. Damping is the only thing preventing infinite growth. Without it, resonance would shake any system apart.

Beyond Physics

The Resonance Principle. Every system has a natural frequency. Teams, markets, audiences — they all oscillate at some rhythm. When your input matches their natural frequency, small effort creates massive response. This is why timing matters more than volume. A whisper at resonance beats a shout off-frequency. Find the frequency first, then push.

Constructive vs Destructive Interference. When two efforts align in phase, they amplify each other. When they're out of phase, they cancel. This is why alignment matters more than raw talent. Ten brilliant engineers out of phase produce less than five average engineers in sync. Culture isn't about values on a wall — it's about phase alignment.

The Damping Paradox. Some damping prevents catastrophe. The Tacoma Narrows Bridge collapsed because it had too little damping — resonance grew unchecked. In organizations, feedback loops and healthy skepticism are damping. Remove all friction and you get runaway hype cycles, unchecked spending, or echo chambers. The right amount of damping keeps the system alive.
