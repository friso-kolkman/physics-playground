The Physics

Entropy measures disorder, or more precisely, the number of ways a system can be arranged while looking the same from the outside. The second law of thermodynamics says the entropy of a closed system never decreases. It can stay the same or go up, but it never spontaneously goes down.

When you remove the partition, particles spread to fill the entire box. This is irreversible in practice. Statistically, the particles will not spontaneously return to one side. With more particles, the probability of reversal becomes so astronomically small that it effectively never happens in the lifetime of the universe.

Math Details

Boltzmann's entropy formula:

S = kB ln(Ω)

Where Ω is the number of microstates (distinct arrangements). For N particles that can each be on the left or right half:

Ω = 2N total arrangements

But only 1 arrangement has all particles on the left side. The probability of spontaneous return:

P = (1/2)N

For 100 particles, that is roughly 10-30. For 200 particles: 10-60. Effectively impossible.

Beyond Physics

The Partition Test. Before any major action, ask: "Am I removing a partition?" Information shared can't be unshared. Layoffs can't be un-announced. Code open-sourced can't be un-released. For reversible decisions, move fast. For partition-removal decisions, move carefully.

The fix isn't to prevent entropy (impossible) but to budget energy for constant maintenance. Organizations that stop investing in structure slowly dissolve into disorder.

The Second Law of Startups. In any system, disorder increases unless you add energy. This applies to: team alignment, code quality, customer relationships, brand perception. If you stop actively maintaining something, entropy guarantees its decay. Budget for maintenance or accept the decline.
