The Physics

Ohm’s law is the foundation of circuit analysis: voltage equals current times resistance, V = IR. It tells you that for a given resistance, pushing harder (more voltage) drives more current, and for a given voltage, more resistance means less current. It’s a simple linear relationship, but it governs everything from phone chargers to power grids.

In a series circuit, components share the same current but divide the voltage. The total resistance is simply the sum of all resistors — each one adds to the bottleneck. In a parallel circuit, components share the same voltage but divide the current. Each branch provides an alternative path, so the total resistance is always less than the smallest individual resistor. Current naturally distributes itself so that more flows through lower-resistance paths.

Power dissipation follows P = IV, which can be rewritten as P = I²R or P = V²/R. The V² form reveals something important: doubling voltage quadruples power. This is why high-voltage transmission lines lose less energy — for the same power delivered, higher voltage means lower current and thus less I²R heating in the wires.

Math Details

Ohm’s law — the fundamental circuit equation:

V = I R

Series resistance — resistances add directly:

Rtotal = R&sub1; + R&sub2; + R&sub3;

Parallel resistance — reciprocals add:

1/Rtotal = 1/R&sub1; + 1/R&sub2; + 1/R&sub3;

Power dissipation — three equivalent forms:

P = IV = I²R = V²/R

Current conservation at a junction (Kirchhoff’s current law):

Σ Iin = Σ Iout

Beyond Physics

Ohm’s Law for Organizations. Reducing resistance (bureaucracy, unclear ownership, approval chains) is often more effective than increasing voltage (pressure, urgency, longer hours). A small reduction in organizational resistance can unlock far more throughput than any amount of pushing harder. Fix the process before blaming the people.

Series vs Parallel Teams. A series workflow creates bottlenecks — the slowest step sets the pace for everything downstream. A parallel structure multiplies throughput by letting independent streams run simultaneously. The best organizations ruthlessly identify which dependencies are real (must be series) and which are artificial (can be parallelized). Most bottlenecks are organizational, not technical.

Power Scales with V². Intensity and effort scale nonlinearly. Doubling your commitment to something doesn’t double the output — it can quadruple it, because focus compounds. But the same V² law applies to burnout: doubling the voltage on a system quadruples the heat dissipated. Know which resistors in your life are generating useful work and which are just burning energy.
